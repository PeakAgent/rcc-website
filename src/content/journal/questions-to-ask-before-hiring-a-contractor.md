By the time most disputes reach me, the answer was available at the very
beginning — it just was never asked for. Choosing a contractor is the single
most consequential decision in a project, and it is usually made on the basis of
a price and a good first impression. Here are five questions that reveal far
more than either.

## 1. "Can I see the schedule, not just the start date?"

Anyone can name a start date. A real schedule shows the sequence — when trades
arrive, what they depend on, and where the critical path runs. A contractor who
can't produce one hasn't planned the job; they're going to plan it on your time.

## 2. "How do you handle change orders?"

The answer should be specific and in writing: how changes are priced, who
approves them, and when. "We'll work it out as we go" is the most expensive
sentence in construction.

## 3. "Who, exactly, is on site every day?"

The person who sells the job is rarely the person who runs it. Ask who your
day-to-day point of contact will be, how many other jobs they're carrying, and
how often the principal actually visits.

## 4. "What does your bid exclude?"

Every bid has exclusions; cheap bids simply hide them. Reading what a bid
*leaves out* tells you more about the final cost than the number on the cover
page. This is where an owner's representative earns their keep.

## 5. "Can I speak with a client from a job that went sideways?"

Anyone can supply a happy reference. The more useful question is how a contractor
behaves when a project hits trouble — because every project eventually does. The
willingness to answer this question honestly is itself the answer.

---

You don't have to ask these alone. Part of representing an owner is sitting in
these conversations with you, hearing what's said, and noticing what isn't.
