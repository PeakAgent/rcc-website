A renovation budget is not a number. It is a set of decisions, most of which get
made before a single wall comes down. The projects that stay on budget are
almost always the ones that were scoped honestly at the start — and in the
Coachella Valley, honest scoping means accounting for the desert itself.

## Start with the building, not the finishes

It is natural to begin with the things you can see: the kitchen, the floors, the
pool deck. But the budget is decided by the things you can't — the condition of
the roof, the age of the mechanical systems, the way a mid-century home was
originally wired and insulated for a climate we now ask far more of.

We start every estimate with the bones. A beautiful finish over a tired system
is the most expensive thing you can build, because you pay for it twice.

## The desert tax

Building in the Coachella Valley carries real, specific costs:

- **Cooling loads.** HVAC sized for 115-degree summers is not optional, and
  retrofitting an older home to carry it properly is rarely cheap.
- **Sun and material movement.** Intense UV and large temperature swings are
  hard on finishes, sealants, and anything that expands and contracts.
- **Water and landscape.** Drought-conscious design is both a value and a
  constraint, and it interacts with grading, drainage, and hardscape budgets.

None of this is a reason not to build. It is a reason to plan.

## Build the contingency in, not on top

A contingency is not padding — it is a planning instrument. On renovation work,
where the walls always hold a surprise or two, we plan a contingency
appropriate to how much of the existing structure remains unknown. The goal is
that a discovery becomes a line item, not a crisis.

## Decide before you dig

The most expensive change is the one made after the work has started. Selections,
allowances, and scope should be settled while they still live on paper. Every
decision deferred into the construction phase is a decision that will cost more
and slow things down.

A good budget, in the end, is just a good set of decisions made early — and
held to.
